This Asset Map provides an overview of various assets that exist with the sport pipeline that contribute to the prevention of sexual violence. While this is not an exhaustive list of resources that currently exist, it does provide us with a better overall view of where there is traction and where there are gaps within the sport system to prevent sexual violence, both within sport and through sport as a societal influencer.

About the Sport Pipeline: The 'sport pipeline' represents the stages at which individual and sport systems are built to support a particular developmental stage in sport (ex. youth leagues, high school teams, collegiate athletic programs, and profession clubs). Additionally, the sport system is organized in national, state or regional, and local levels, with independent organizations working in direct or indirect with other organizations at different levels.

About the Assets to Prevent Sexual Violence: The assets named in the sport systems aim to prevent sexual violence within the sport system and/or aim to prevent sexual violence overall by engaging the sport system and it's influence. We are identified 7 key assets that aim to prevent sexual violence that exist within with the sport community; 1) Policy, 2) Training and Tools, 3) Resources, 4) Champion Building, 5) Communities of Practice, 6) Partnerships, and 7) Evaluation. These assets were identified through key informant interviews with stakeholders in both the sexual and domestic violence prevention field and individuals working in sport organization, a Sport Think Tank, an Advocate Think Tank, and Focus Groups with the sport community at each stage of the sport pipeline. Assets that are listed do not necessarily represent evidence-based or best practice, but rather shows the depth and breadth of strategies, programs, resources, etc. that are active with in the sport pipeline to prevent sexual violence.
